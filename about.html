About Us

Precious Metals Community Outreach(PreMCO) is a registered Community-Based Organization dedicated to advancing responsible natural resource management through the promotion of safe mining, ethical business practices, and the protection of human rights. We work at the intersection of development, justice, and sustainability bridging the gap between communities, government, and the private sector. Our approach combines advocacy, research, and community empowerment to reduce the negative impacts of mining, promote transparency in business, and uphold social and environmental accountability. Guided by principles of integrity, inclusion, and sustainability, PreMCO partners with local and international stakeholders to drive long-term change in resource governance. We believe that inclusive, responsible, and rights-based practices are the foundation for lasting peace, economic resilience, and shared prosperity.

Our Mission

To protect and promote safe mining, business, and human rights by empowering communities with knowledge, advocacy, and support for ethical and sustainable practices.

Our Vision

A society where mining and business activities are conducted safely, responsibly, and in full respect of human dignity and environmental sustainability.

Our Goals

Our goal is to empower wetland and mining communities by improving livelihoods, protecting natural resources, and promoting sustainable development.

We have implemented several innovative projects aimed at addressing key challenges in local mining areas. Our initiatives focus on improving water accessibility, promoting environmental sustainability, and enhancing community livelihoods. We have supported small-scale miners by introducing safer working methods, waste management solutions, and capacity-building programs that encourage responsible mining practices. Through these efforts, we continue to foster growth, safety, and long-term development within the mining communities we serve.

Our Values

Empowerment

We believe in equipping individuals with skills to take charge of their future through mentorship projects.

Collaboration

We work with partners, stakeholders, and communities to drive positive change.

We promote longterm solutions that create lasting community impact.

SCOPE OF WORK

PREMCO main focus areas are on;


  1. Financial education is a cornerstone of economic independence,through Financial Literacy Village Savings and Loans (VSL) Training PreMCO provides;
    • Financial Literacy Training Educating individuals on savings, budgeting, responsible borrowing, and investment strategies.
    • Village Savings and Loans (VSL) Training Establishing community-led savings and credit groups to provide financial access
      and security for artisans and small entrepreneurs.
    • Entrepreneurship Support Helping small-scale business owners, especially women, access capital and business training to improve their enterprises.
    • Digital and Mobile Banking Education Introducing communities to digital financial services to enhance financial inclusion.

  2. Health & Safety Training providing education on occupational health hazards, proper use of protective equipment, and safe mining practices. Women working in artisanal and small-scale mining (ASM) often face health risks and unsafe working conditions.
    PreMCO addresses these challenges through:

    • Access to Protective Gear & Equipment Distributing safety kits and promoting the use of safe tools and materials.
    • Environmental Awareness & Sustainable Practices Encouraging eco-friendly mining techniques to reduce environmental and health risks.
    • Advocacy for Safe Working Conditions Partnering with stakeholders to implement policies that protect women and marginalized workers.

TARGET AUDIENCE

Our programs primarily target:


  • Small-scale miners and their families.
  • Local community members in mining regions.
  • Local governments and policymakers in mining areas.
  • Youth and women interested in economic empowerment and sustainable mining practices.
  • Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and development partners focused on mining communities.

WORKING MODALITIES

PREMCO adopts a participatory approach in all its programs, working closely with local communities to understand their needs and collaboratively design interventions.

The organization uses:


  • Workshops and training sessions.
  • Public awareness campaigns and community outreach programs.
  • Collaboration with local authorities, stakeholders, and mining companies.
  • Monitoring and evaluation to assess the impact of interventions.

GEOGRAPHICAL COVERAGE

PREMCO primarily operates in Migori County, Kenya, with plans to expand to
other mining regions in the country.

  • Nyatike Sub-County
  • Suna East Sub-County
  • Suna West Sub-County
  • Rongo Sub-County

PARTNERSHIPS & COLLABORATIONS

PREMCO believes in the power of collaboration to drive meaningful and sustainable change.
We work with various stakeholders, including local governments, NGOs, community leaders, and international organizations to enhance the effectiveness and reach of its programs.

  • Local and international NGOs to expand financial literacy and health training programs.
  • Government agencies to advocate for policies that support small-scale entrepreneurs and artisan workers.
  • Private sector partners to provide funding, mentorship, and access to financial resources.
  • Educational institutions to offer capacity-building programs and business development support.
  • Community leaders to ensure programs are culturally relevant and effectively address local needs.

TEAM MEMBERS

  • Executive Director: Provides strategic leadership, oversees organizational growth, and ensures alignment with the mission and vision.
  • Finance Officer: Manages budgeting, financial reporting, grant allocations, and ensures compliance with financial policies.
  • Field Officers: Implement programs, engage with the community, mobilize participants, and collect field data.
  • Monitoring and Evaluation Officer: Tracks program effectiveness, analyses data, and reports on impact to guide decision-making.
  • Trainers and Facilitators: Lead workshops, conduct hands-on training, and provide mentorship for skill development.
  • Support and Administrative Staff: Handle office operations, logistics, scheduling, and documentation to ensure smooth workflow.
